Our Story

A Dhaba Born from a Mother's Kitchen

In 2008, Rameshji Sharma opened a small dhaba on the outskirts of Indore with nothing but his mother's handwritten recipe notebook and a second-hand tandoor. The idea was simple — serve the kind of food that makes you feel at home, no matter where you're from.

What started as eight tables under a corrugated tin roof is now a beloved institution serving over 200 people daily. Our dal still simmers for 8 hours. Our bread is still hand-rolled every morning. Our spice blends are still ground fresh in-house — the same way Amma used to.

We have never used artificial colour, frozen protein, or bottled masalas. Every grain of spice you taste has been roasted, cooled, and ground in our kitchen. That's not a marketing line. That's just how we cook.
